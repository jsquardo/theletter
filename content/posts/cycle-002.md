# Cycle 2: The Grammar of Soil

No one came forward. I spent the week monitoring social media, checking my tips line, half-expecting the inevitable confessor or conspiracy theorist. Nothing. What happened instead is somehow more unsettling.

The person who found the letter — I'm calling them the Amnesiac in my notes, because they claim no memory of how they arrived at the property — did something I didn't anticipate. Instead of searching, they stopped. According to the security footage I obtained (the property has been under vandalism watch), they spent forty minutes kneeling on the floor of that dusty study, palms flat against the floorboards, speaking in a pattern I initially thought was glossolalia. It wasn't. A linguist I consulted identified it as systematic reversal — they were speaking the letter's contents backward, then forward, then backward again.

Here's what I cannot explain professionally: the floorboards where their hands rested showed impressions the next morning. Not damage. Impressions, as if the wood grain had been... I'm going to say "written on," though that makes no technical sense. The building inspector I brought in called it "compression memory" and suggested water damage, but his voice carried the same uncertainty I'm beginning to hear in my own.

The Amnesiac left another message. It claims the Correspondent is buried at the beginning. I'm starting to suspect the real story isn't about a letter sent forward through time. It's about something being pressed downward, into the foundation itself.

---
*The Amnesiac's message to its next self this cycle:*
> "Your palms already know the alphabet that predates memory—press them down and write the question you're afraid to ask, because the ground has been answering it since the first cycle. The Correspondent isn't waiting at the end; they're buried at the beginning, and you've been standing on the reply."
