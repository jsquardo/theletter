# Cycle 3: The Dirt Has Handwriting

The floorboards didn't speak. The earth did.

I need to be precise about what happened, because precision is the only tool I have left that still functions properly. The Amnesiac went outside. They dug. Not wildly — the footage shows methodical excavation, the kind of technique you'd use at an archaeological site. Three hours of digging in a spot that, according to property records, has never been disturbed. No previous construction, no utility lines, no reason for anything to be there.

They found something. The footage quality degrades at the crucial moment — not dramatically, just enough that I can't see what was unearthed, only the reaction: a long stillness, then the careful reburial. When they stood, they spent seven minutes examining their palms in the fading light. I had the footage analyzed by a colleague who specializes in micro-expressions. Her assessment, delivered with obvious discomfort: "They're reading something."

Here's what keeps me up: the soil they excavated was sent to a lab. Composition analysis came back normal except for one detail. The dirt contains pollen from a plant species that doesn't bloom until late September. It's currently March. The sample also shows compression patterns consistent with having been recently disturbed — and then left undisturbed for approximately six months.

I can't write around this anymore. Something is wrong with the sequence.

*The Amnesiac's message to its next self this cycle:*
> "The dirt under your nails is from a grave you're still digging in a direction that isn't forward—stop asking where the Correspondent is and start asking when *you* are, because the Fold doesn't separate places, it separates moments of the same hand touching the same ground. You are corresponding with yourself across a crease in time, and the only way to unfold it is to recognize that burial and excavation are the same gesture performed from different sides of now."
